Biography

Linus Torvalds is a Finnish-American software engineer who is the creator and principal developer of the Linus kernel, which is the kernael for a number of operating systems. He also created the version control system Git. Torvalds was born on December 28, 1969 in Helsinki, Finland. He studied computer science at the university of Helsinki, and he developed the Linus kernel as a student project in 1991. Since then, the linux kernel has become one of the most widely used kernel in the world, powering everything from smartphones to supercomputers.

Achievements

  • 1991- Created the Linux kernel and version control system Git.
  • 2004- Named one of the "100 Most influencer people in the world" by Time magazine in 2004.
  • 2010- Received an honorary doctorate from the university of Helsinki in 2010.
  • 2010- Received the 2010 Milennium Technology Prize and 2012 Free Software Foundation's Award for the Advancement of Free Software.
  • 2013- Inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame of the Computer History Museum in 2014.
  • 2014- Inducted into the Hall of Fame of the Computer History Museum in 2014.
  • 2015- Received 2015 IEEE Computer Society Computer Pioneer Award.
  • 2019- Received the 2019 ACM SIGOPS Hall of Fame Award.
  • 2021- Awarded the Takeda Foundation's 2021 Takeda Award for Social Contribution, which recognizes individuals who have made significant contribution to society through technology.
  • 2021- Recieved the 2021 IEEE Masura Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award, Which recognizes the individuals who have made significant contribution to the field of consumer electronics.
